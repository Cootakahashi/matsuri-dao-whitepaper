Ethereum is the pioneer of blockchains that brought smart contracts to the world. It opened up the cultures of DeFi and NFTs; the **security and reliability** built by its massive developer community and long track record are still unmatched in the industry.

But what MTC wants to realize is "everyday payments on the ground at tourism sites." The act of tossing 100 yen (~$0.70) into the offering box does not tolerate fees of hundreds of yen or waits of tens of seconds.

---

## Comparison: Ethereum vs Solana

| Item | Ethereum | Solana |
| :--- | :---: | :---: |
| **Transfer fee** | Hundreds to thousands of yen (~$2–$20) | **~¥0.04 (~$0.0003)** |
| **Settlement speed** | 12 seconds to several minutes | **0.4 seconds** |
| **Throughput (per second)** | ~15 | **Thousands** |
| **Strength** | High-value transactions, DeFi, security-critical use | **High-frequency, micro-payment, real-time experience** |

![Tortoise and Shinkansen — Ethereum's steady gait, Solana's overwhelming speed](/brand/08_A_tortoise_shinkansen.webp)

Without the foundation Ethereum built, neither Solana nor today's Web3 would exist.
On top of that, what can meet the requirements of **real tourism demand × high-frequency micro-settlement** is, at this moment, only Solana.
